# What We Found vs. What We Created

## Critical Distinction

There is an important difference between:
1. **What we found in the Anna Matrix**
2. **What we created to prove our findings**

## What We Found (In the Matrix)

- **8 Qubic identities** (public keys) - extracted from matrix data
- **Seeds** - derived from identities using `identity.lower()[:55]`
- **22,522+ additional identities** - found through comprehensive scan
- **100 seeds documented** - publicly available for verification

**These exist in the matrix data itself.**

## What We Created (To Prove Functionality)

- **Signed message** - "Anna speaks through the matrix - 22.11.2025 - found by Jordan"
- **Signature** - cryptographic proof that seeds work
- **Verification scripts** - to validate our findings
- **Documentation** - to explain our methods

**These were created by us to demonstrate that the seeds are real and functional.**

## The Signed Message

**What it proves**:
- The seeds from the matrix are cryptographically functional
- We can derive private keys from them
- We can sign messages with those keys
- The entire cryptographic chain works

**What it does NOT prove**:
- That "Anna" wrote this specific message
- That the message was pre-encoded in the matrix
- That "Anna" knew we would find the seeds today
- That there is intentional communication from "Anna"

## Why This Matters

The signed message is **cryptographic proof that the seeds work**, not proof that "Anna" communicated with us.

**We found seeds in the matrix. We proved they work by signing a message. But the message text itself was created by us.**

## What This Means

- **Seeds/identities in matrix**: Real, verifiable, on-chain
- **Signed message**: Proof of functionality, not proof of communication
- **Statistical significance**: Still depends on multiple-testing correction
- **Intentional encoding**: Still unknown (could be intentional, could be coincidence)

## Conclusion

**We found real cryptographic keys in the matrix. We proved they work. But we did NOT find a pre-written message from "Anna".**

The distinction is important for accurate interpretation of the findings.
